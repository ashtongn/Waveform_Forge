How a receiver interprets a digital frame

From raw bits to useful information

A receiver does not see a message all at once. It detects timing, finds a known synchronization pattern, reads addressing and control information, extracts the payload, verifies integrity, and recognizes the end of the frame.

Receive flow

A simplified view of what the receiver is doing internally.

01
Detect energy and timing

The receiver identifies a signal and establishes symbol or bit timing.

02
Find synchronization

A known pattern marks a reliable alignment point in the incoming stream.

03
Interpret control fields

Identifiers, lengths, types, and flags tell the receiver how to handle the frame.

04
Extract and verify data

The payload is recovered and checked for corruption before being accepted.
